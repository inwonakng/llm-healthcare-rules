1. The policy covers injectable osteoporosis drugs for Medicare beneficiaries.
2. The beneficiary must be a woman with osteoporosis who qualifies for the Medicare home health benefit.
3. The beneficiary must have a bone fracture that a doctor certifies is related to post-menopausal osteoporosis.
4. The beneficiary's doctor must certify that the beneficiary is unable to self-administer the injection or learn how to self-administer the drug.
5. Medicare will also cover the services of a home health nurse or aide to administer the injection if the beneficiary's family or caregivers are unable or unwilling to administer the drug.
