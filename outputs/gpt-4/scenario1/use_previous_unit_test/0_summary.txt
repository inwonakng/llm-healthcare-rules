1. The policy covers injectable osteoporosis drugs.
2. The coverage is applicable if the patient is a woman with osteoporosis who qualifies for the Medicare home health benefit.
3. The patient must have a bone fracture that a doctor certifies was related to post-menopausal osteoporosis.
4. The doctor must certify that the patient cannot self-administer the injection or learn how to self-administer the drug.
5. Medicare will also cover the services of a home health nurse or aide to provide the injection if the patient's family or caregivers are unable or unwilling to administer the drug by injection.
