1. Medicare will cover the cost of injectable osteoporosis drugs under certain conditions. 
2. The person receiving the medication must be a woman with osteoporosis who qualifies for the Medicare home health benefit. 
3. The woman must have suffered a bone fracture that a doctor confirms was caused by post-menopausal osteoporosis.
4. A doctor must certify that the woman is incapable of administering the injection to herself and cannot be taught to do so.
5. If the conditions in requirements 2 to 4 are met, Medicare will also cover the cost of a home health nurse or aide to provide the injection. This is only applicable if the family or caregivers cannot or do not want to administer the drug by injection.
