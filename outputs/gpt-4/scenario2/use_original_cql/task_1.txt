1. Medicare covers Intravenous Immune Globulin (IVIG) if the patient has a diagnosis of primary immune deficiency disease.
2. The provision of IVIG at home must be deemed medically appropriate by the patient's doctor.
3. Medicare Part B is responsible for the coverage of IVIG itself.
4. Medicare Part B does not cover additional items and services related to the administration of IVIG at home.
