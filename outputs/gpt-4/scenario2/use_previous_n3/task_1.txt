1. Intravenous Immune Globulin (IVIG) can be covered by Medicare if administered at home.
2. To avail the cover, the patient must have a diagnosis of primary immune deficiency disease.
3. The decision to administer IVIG in-home should be medically appropriate, as decided by the patient's doctor.
4. Medicare's Part B insurance covers the cost of the IVIG itself.
5. Despite requirement 4, Part B insurance does not cover other items and services related to the patient receiving the IVIG at home.
