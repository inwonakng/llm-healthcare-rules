1. The insurance policy covers Intravenous Immune Globulin (IVIG) if it is provided at home.
2. The coverage is only applicable if the insured has a diagnosis of primary immune deficiency disease.
3. The decision of whether it is medically appropriate for the insured to receive IVIG at home must be made by a doctor.
4. The insurance policy under Part B covers the cost of the IVIG itself.
5. The policy does not cover the cost of other items and services related to the insured receiving the IVIG at home.
