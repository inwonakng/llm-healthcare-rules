High-level summary: The text explains the coverage provided by Medicare for transplant drug therapy, including the requirements and costs associated with it.

Requirements for Medicare coverage of transplant drug therapy:

1. Medicare must have helped pay for your organ transplant.
2. You must have Part A coverage at the time of the covered transplant.
3. You must have Part B coverage at the time you receive immunosuppressive drugs.
4. If Part B doesn't cover immunosuppressive drugs, Medicare Part D (drug coverage) can cover them.
5. If you have Original Medicare, you can join a Medicare drug plan for coverage.
6. For individuals with Medicare due to End-Stage Renal Disease (ESRD), coverage ends 36 months after a successful kidney transplant.
7. If you lose Part A coverage 36 months after a kidney transplant and don't have certain types of other health coverage, Medicare offers a benefit that covers immunosuppressive drugs only.
8. This benefit is not a substitute for full health coverage.
9. You can sign up for this benefit after your Part A coverage ends by contacting Social Security.
10. TTY users can call 1-800-325-0788 for assistance.

Costs for the immunosuppressive drug benefit in 2023:

11. A monthly premium of $97.10*.
12. $226 deductible.
13. After meeting the deductible, you'll pay 20% of the Medicare-approved amount for immunosuppressive drugs.

Note: The requirements for coverage should be met to qualify for the benefit, and the associated costs apply to the immunosuppressive drug benefit.
