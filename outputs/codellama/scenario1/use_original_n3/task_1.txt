Outline: Injectable osteoporosis drugs covered by Medicare

1. Patient must be a woman with osteoporosis who meets the criteria for the Medicare home health benefit.
2. Patient must have a bone fracture that a doctor certifies was related to post-menopausal osteoporosis.
3. Doctor must certify that patient can't give themselves the injection or learn how to give themselves the drug by injection.
4. Medicare will cover the home health nurse or aide to provide the injection if the patient's family or caregivers are unable or unwilling to give the drug by injection.
5. Patients with osteoporosis who meet these conditions can receive injectable osteoporosis drugs covered by Medicare.

Note: The requirements in the document have been summarized in a numbered list format, and each requirement has been described on a separate line. The final decision is explicitly described at the end of the outline.
