Summary: Medicare covers intravenous immune globulin (IVIG) if the patient has a diagnosis of primary immune deficiency disease and the physician considers it medically necessary. Part B covers the IVIG but does not pay for additional goods and services associated with receiving IVIG at home.

Requirements:

1. The patient must have a diagnosis of primary immune deficiency disease.
2. The physician must consider it medically necessary for the patient to receive IVIG.
3. The patient must receive IVIG at home.
4. Medicare covers the IVIG.
5. Part B of Medicare covers the IVIG but does not cover other items and services related to receiving IVIG at home.
