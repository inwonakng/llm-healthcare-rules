Medicare covers Intravenous Immune Globulin (IVIG) if the patient has a diagnosis of primary immune deficiency disease and the physician determines that it is medically necessary for the patient to receive the IVIG in their home. Part B covers the cost of the IVIG itself, but does not cover any additional costs associated with administering the IVIG in the patient's home.

Requirements:

1. The patient must have a diagnosis of primary immune deficiency disease.
2. The patient's physician must determine that it is medically necessary for them to receive IVIG in their home.
3. Part B covers the cost of the IVIG itself.
4. Part B does not cover any additional costs associated with administering the IVIG in the patient's home.
