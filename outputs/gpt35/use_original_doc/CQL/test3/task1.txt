High-level summary: Medicare provides coverage for transplant drug therapy, including immunosuppressive drugs, under certain conditions. The coverage is dependent on having Medicare Parts A and B, and there are options available for obtaining Medicare drug coverage.

Requirements:
1. Medicare covers transplant drug therapy if Medicare helped pay for the organ transplant.
2. Part A coverage is required at the time of the covered transplant.
3. Part B coverage is required at the time of receiving immunosuppressive drugs.
4. If Part B doesn't cover immunosuppressive drugs, Medicare Part D (drug coverage) can cover them.
5. Original Medicare beneficiaries can join a Medicare drug plan for drug coverage.
6. Medicare coverage, including immunosuppressive drug coverage, ends 36 months after a successful kidney transplant for individuals with Medicare solely due to End-Stage Renal Disease (ESRD).
7. Medicare offers a benefit to help individuals who lose Part A coverage 36 months after a kidney transplant and don't have certain other health coverage.
8. The benefit only covers immunosuppressive drugs and not other items or services.
9. The benefit is not a substitute for full health coverage.
10. Individuals can sign up for this benefit after their Part A coverage ends by contacting Social Security.
11. The monthly premium for the immunosuppressive drug benefit in 2023 is $97.10*.
12. A $226 deductible applies if individuals sign up for the immunosuppressive drug benefit.
13. After meeting the deductible, individuals will pay 20% of the Medicare-approved amount for their immunosuppressive drugs.

*The asterisk indicates a footnote or additional information that may not be explicitly provided in the given text.
