High-level summary: Medicare covers Intravenous Immune Globulin (IVIG) provided at home for individuals diagnosed with primary immune deficiency disease, as long as their doctor determines it is medically necessary. However, Medicare only covers the IVIG itself and does not cover other related items and services.

Requirements:
1. Medicare coverage for IVIG at home is available for individuals with primary immune deficiency disease.
2. The decision to receive IVIG at home must be made by the individual's doctor.
3. Medicare Part B covers the cost of IVIG.
4. Medicare does not cover other items and services associated with receiving IVIG at home.
