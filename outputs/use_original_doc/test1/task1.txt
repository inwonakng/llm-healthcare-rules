Summary: Medicare covers injectable osteoporosis drugs for eligible women with osteoporosis who meet specific conditions, including qualifying for the Medicare home health benefit, having a doctor-certified bone fracture related to post-menopausal osteoporosis, and being unable to self-administer the injection or learn how to do so. Additionally, Medicare will cover a home health nurse or aide to provide the injection if family or caregivers are unable or unwilling to do so.

Requirements:

1. To be eligible for Medicare coverage of injectable osteoporosis drugs, you must be a woman with osteoporosis who qualifies for the Medicare home health benefit.
2. Your doctor must certify that your bone fracture is related to post-menopausal osteoporosis.
3. Your doctor must certify that you are unable to administer the injection to yourself or learn how to do so.
4. If your family or caregivers are unable or unwilling to administer the injection, Medicare will cover a home health nurse or aide to provide it.

Final decision: Medicare will cover injectable osteoporosis drugs and, if necessary, the services of a home health nurse or aide for eligible individuals who meet the specified conditions.
