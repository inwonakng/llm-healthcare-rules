Summary: Medicare covers transplant drug therapy if Medicare helped pay for your organ transplant. There are certain requirements and options for receiving coverage, and specific costs associated with the drug benefit.

Requirements:

1. You must have Part A at the time of the covered transplant.
2. You must have Part B at the time you get immunosuppressive drugs.
3. If Part B doesn't cover immunosuppressive drugs, Medicare drug coverage (Part D) can be obtained.
4. Original Medicare beneficiaries can join a Medicare drug plan to get drug coverage.
5. Medicare coverage, including immunosuppressive drug coverage, ends 36 months after a successful kidney transplant for individuals with Medicare due to End-Stage Renal Disease (ESRD).
6. Medicare offers a benefit for immunosuppressive drugs if certain conditions are met, such as losing Part A coverage 36 months after a kidney transplant and not having other specific health coverage.
7. The benefit only covers immunosuppressive drugs and doesn't substitute for full health coverage.
8. If eligible, individuals can sign up for this benefit after their Part A coverage ends by contacting Social Security.
9. In 2023, the monthly premium for the immunosuppressive drug benefit is $97.10* and there is a $226 deductible.
10. After meeting the deductible, individuals will pay 20% of the Medicare-approved amount for their immunosuppressive drugs.

*Final Decision: Individuals will need to pay a monthly premium of $97.10 for the immunosuppressive drug benefit in 2023.
