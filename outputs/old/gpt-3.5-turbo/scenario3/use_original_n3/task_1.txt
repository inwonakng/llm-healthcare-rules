"Summary:
Medicare covers transplant drug therapy if Medicare helped pay for your organ transplant. There are certain requirements to receive this coverage. Additionally, Medicare offers a benefit for individuals who lose Part A coverage 36 months after a kidney transplant and do not have certain types of other health coverage. In 2023, there is a monthly premium and deductible for the immunosuppressive drug benefit."

Requirements:

1. Medicare must have helped pay for your organ transplant.
2. You must have Part A at the time of the covered transplant.
3. You must have Part B at the time you get immunosuppressive drugs.
4. If Part B doesn't cover immunosuppressive drugs, Medicare drug coverage (Part D) covers them.
5. If you have Original Medicare, you may join a Medicare drug plan for drug coverage.
6. If you have Medicare only due to End-Stage Renal Disease (ESRD), your coverage ends 36 months after a successful kidney transplant.
7. Medicare offers a benefit for individuals who lose Part A coverage 36 months after a kidney transplant and don't have certain types of other health coverage.
8. This benefit only covers immunosuppressive drugs and not other items or services.
9. It is not a substitute for full health coverage.
10. To sign up for the benefit, call Social Security.
11. In 2023, there is a monthly premium of $97.10 and a $226 deductible for the immunosuppressive drug benefit.
12. After meeting the deductible, you'll pay 20% of the Medicare-approved amount for immunosuppressive drugs.
