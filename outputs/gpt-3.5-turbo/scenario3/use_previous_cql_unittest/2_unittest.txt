### CQL Named Expressions
```
1. MedicareHelpedPayForOrganTransplant: Medicare helped pay for your organ transplant.
2. HasPartAAtTimeOfTransplant: You must have Part A at the time of the covered transplant.
3. HasPartBAtTimeOfImmunosuppressiveDrugs: You must have Part B at the time you get immunosuppressive drugs.
4. PartDCoversImmunosuppressiveDrugs: Medicare drug coverage (Part D) covers immunosuppressive drugs if Part B doesn’t cover them.
5. CanJoinMedicareDrugPlan: If you have Original Medicare, you may join a Medicare drug plan to get Medicare drug coverage.
6. MedicareCoverageEndsAfter36Months: If you only have Medicare because of End-Stage Renal Disease (ESRD), your Medicare coverage, including immunosuppressive drug coverage, ends 36 months after a successful kidney transplant.
7. MedicareOffersBenefit: Medicare offers a benefit that may help you if you lose Part A coverage 36 months after a kidney transplant and you don’t have certain types of other health coverage.
8. BenefitCoversOnlyImmunosuppressiveDrugs: This benefit only covers your immunosuppressive drugs and no other items or services.
9. BenefitIsNotSubstituteForFullCoverage: It isn’t a substitute for full health coverage.
10. CanSignUpForBenefit: If you qualify, you can sign up for this benefit any time after your Part A coverage ends.
11. CallSocialSecurityToSignUp: To sign up, call Social Security at 1-877-465-0355. TTY users can call 1-800-325-0788.
12. MonthlyPremiumIn2023: In 2023, the monthly premium for the immunosuppressive drug benefit is $97.10*.
13. DeductibleIn2023: In 2023, there is a $226 deductible for the immunosuppressive drug benefit.
14. Pay20PercentAfterDeductible: Once you've met the deductible, you'll pay 20% of the Medicare-approved amount for your immunosuppressive drugs.
