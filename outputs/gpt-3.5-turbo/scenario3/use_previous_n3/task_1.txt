High-level summary: Medicare provides coverage for transplant drug therapy, including immunosuppressive drugs, if Medicare helped pay for the organ transplant. There are certain requirements and options for getting this coverage.

Requirements for Medicare coverage of transplant drug therapy:
1. Have Part A coverage at the time of the covered transplant.
2. Have Part B coverage at the time of receiving immunosuppressive drugs.
3. If Part B doesn't cover immunosuppressive drugs, Medicare drug coverage (Part D) can be used.
4. If you have Original Medicare, you can join a Medicare drug plan for drug coverage.
5. If you only have Medicare due to End-Stage Renal Disease (ESRD), Medicare coverage, including immunosuppressive drugs, ends 36 months after a successful kidney transplant.
6. Medicare offers a benefit if you lose Part A coverage 36 months after a kidney transplant and don't have certain other health coverage. This benefit only covers immunosuppressive drugs and not other services.
7. The benefit is not a substitute for full health coverage.
8. To sign up for the benefit, call Social Security at 1-877-465-0355. TTY users can call 1-800-325-0788.

Costs for the immunosuppressive drug benefit in 2023:
- Monthly premium of $97.10*
- $226 deductible
- After meeting the deductible, you'll pay 20% of the Medicare-approved amount for immunosuppressive drugs.
