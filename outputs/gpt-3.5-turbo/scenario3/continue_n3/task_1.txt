High-level summary: The text provides information about Medicare coverage for transplant drug therapy, specifically for individuals who received organ transplants.

Requirements:
1. Medicare covers transplant drug therapy if Medicare helped pay for your organ transplant.
2. You must have Part A and Part B at specific times for coverage.
3. Medicare drug coverage (Part D) covers immunosuppressive drugs if Part B doesn't cover them.
4. If you have Original Medicare, you can join a Medicare drug plan for drug coverage.
5. Medicare coverage, including immunosuppressive drug coverage, ends 36 months after a successful kidney transplant for individuals with Medicare due to End-Stage Renal Disease (ESRD).
6. If you lose Part A coverage 36 months after a kidney transplant and don't have certain types of health coverage, Medicare offers a benefit that covers only immunosuppressive drugs.
7. This benefit is not a substitute for full health coverage.
8. Eligible individuals can sign up for this benefit after their Part A coverage ends by calling Social Security.
9. In 2023, the monthly premium for the immunosuppressive drug benefit is $97.10*.
10. There is a $226 deductible for the benefit, and after meeting the deductible, individuals will pay 20% of the Medicare-approved amount for their immunosuppressive drugs.
