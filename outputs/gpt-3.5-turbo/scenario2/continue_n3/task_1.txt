High-level summary: Medicare covers Intravenous Immune Globulin (IVIG) provided in the home for individuals with a diagnosis of primary immune deficiency disease if it is medically appropriate.

Requirements:
1. Medicare coverage for IVIG in the home is available for individuals with a diagnosis of primary immune deficiency disease.
2. The decision to receive IVIG in the home must be made by a doctor.
3. Medicare Part B covers the cost of IVIG itself.
4. Medicare does not cover other items and services related to receiving IVIG at home.

Final decision: Medicare covers IVIG in the home if the individual meets the requirements and it is medically appropriate.
