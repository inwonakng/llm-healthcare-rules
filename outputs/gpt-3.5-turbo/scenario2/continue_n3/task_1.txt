Summary: Medicare covers Intravenous Immune Globulin (IVIG) for individuals diagnosed with primary immune deficiency disease, if it is determined by a doctor to be medically appropriate to receive the IVIG at home. Medicare Part B covers the IVIG itself, but does not cover other items and services related to receiving the IVIG at home.

Requirements:
1. Medicare covers IVIG for individuals diagnosed with primary immune deficiency disease.
2. A doctor must determine that it is medically appropriate for the individual to receive the IVIG at home.
3. Medicare Part B covers the cost of the IVIG itself.
4. Medicare does not cover other items and services related to receiving the IVIG at home.
