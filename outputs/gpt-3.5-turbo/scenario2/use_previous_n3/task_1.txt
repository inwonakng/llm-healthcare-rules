High-level summary: Medicare covers Intravenous Immune Globulin (IVIG) provided at home for individuals with a primary immune deficiency disease, as long as it is deemed medically appropriate by a doctor.

Requirements:
1. Medicare covers IVIG at home for individuals with a primary immune deficiency disease.
2. A doctor must determine that it is medically appropriate for the individual to receive IVIG at home.
3. Medicare Part B covers the cost of the IVIG itself.
4. Medicare does not cover other items and services related to receiving IVIG at home.
