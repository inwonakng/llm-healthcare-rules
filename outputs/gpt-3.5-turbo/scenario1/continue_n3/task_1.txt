High-level summary: Medicare covers injectable osteoporosis drugs for women with osteoporosis who meet certain criteria, including being eligible for the Medicare home health benefit and having a bone fracture related to post-menopausal osteoporosis. If the patient is unable to self-administer the injection or learn how to do so, the doctor must certify it. Additionally, Medicare will cover the cost of a home health nurse or aide to administer the injection if the patient's family or caregivers are unable or unwilling to do so. 

Requirements:

1. Must be a woman with osteoporosis who meets the criteria for the Medicare home health benefit.
2. Must have a bone fracture certified by a doctor as being related to post-menopausal osteoporosis.
3. Doctor must certify that the patient is unable to self-administer the injection or learn how to do so.
4. Medicare will cover the cost of a home health nurse or aide to administer the injection if the patient's family or caregivers are unable or unwilling to do so.
