Summary: Medicare covers injectable osteoporosis drugs for eligible women with osteoporosis who meet specific conditions, including being qualified for the Medicare home health benefit, having a bone fracture related to post-menopausal osteoporosis, and being unable to self-administer the injection or learn how to do so. Additionally, Medicare will cover a home health nurse or aide to administer the injection if the patient's family or caregivers are unable or unwilling to do so.

Requirements:
1. Must be a woman with osteoporosis and meet the criteria for the Medicare home health benefit.
2. Must have a bone fracture certified by a doctor as being related to post-menopausal osteoporosis.
3. Must be certified by a doctor as unable to self-administer the injection or learn how to do so by injection.
4. If family or caregivers are unable or unwilling to provide the injection, Medicare will cover a home health nurse or aide to do so.
