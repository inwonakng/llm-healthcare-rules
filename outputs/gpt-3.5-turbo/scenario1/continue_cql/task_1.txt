Summary: Medicare covers injectable osteoporosis drugs for eligible women with osteoporosis who meet specific criteria, including having a bone fracture related to post-menopausal osteoporosis and being unable to self-administer the injection.

Requirements:
1. Woman with osteoporosis who meets the criteria for the Medicare home health benefit.
2. Bone fracture certified by a doctor to be related to post-menopausal osteoporosis.
3. Inability to self-administer the injection or learn how to do so, certified by the doctor.

Final Decision: Medicare will cover the home health nurse or aide to provide the injection if family or caregivers are unable or unwilling to do so.
