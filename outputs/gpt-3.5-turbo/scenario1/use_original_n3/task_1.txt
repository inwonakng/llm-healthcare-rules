High-level summary: Medicare covers injectable osteoporosis drugs for eligible women with osteoporosis who meet certain conditions, including having a bone fracture related to post-menopausal osteoporosis and being unable to self-administer the injection.

Requirements:
1. Must be a woman with osteoporosis who meets the criteria for the Medicare home health benefit.
2. Must have a bone fracture that is certified by a doctor as being related to post-menopausal osteoporosis.
3. Must have a doctor's certification stating that you are unable to self-administer the injection or learn how to do so.
4. If unable or unwilling, Medicare will cover the cost of a home health nurse or aide to administer the injection.
