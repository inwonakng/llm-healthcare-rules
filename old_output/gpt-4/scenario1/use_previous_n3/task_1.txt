Summary: The document outlines the conditions under which Medicare covers injectable osteoporosis drugs for women. 

1. The patient must be a woman with osteoporosis who qualifies for the Medicare home health benefit.
2. The patient must have suffered a bone fracture that is certified by a doctor to be related to post-menopausal osteoporosis.
3. The patient's doctor should certify that she cannot administer the injection to herself or learn how to do so.
4. Should the patient's family or caregivers be unable or unwilling to administer the drug, Medicare will cover the cost of a home health nurse or aide to provide the injection.
